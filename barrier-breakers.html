Four Innovation Tracks

Choose one challenge. Research why it's a barrier to financial health. Build a solution‚Äîpolicy proposal, app prototype, financial literacy tool, community program, or anything else that could create change.

01

Transportation

Car ownership costs, insurance rates, and public transit gaps prevent young adults from accessing stable jobs and building financial security.

02

Housing Affordability

Rent burden, security deposits, and credit requirements block young adults from stable housing and long-term wealth building opportunities.

Simple Process

Three Steps to Compete

1

Register

Your school signs up teams. Individual students or groups welcome (groups split the cash prizes). Choose one innovation track and start exploring.

2

Research & Build

Spend several weeks understanding the barrier and building your solution. Use AI tools to help prototype apps, analyze data, or create content. Submit 3 weeks before the event. Any format‚Äîvideo demo, working prototype, policy proposal, presentation.

3

Prepare & Present

We'll review submissions and notify you 2 weeks before the event whether you're pitching on stage (7 min + Q&A) or presenting via poster gallery (1-on-1 with judges). Then prepare your presentation format and compete at Kent State.
