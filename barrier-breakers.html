In Partnership with Kent State University

Barrier Breakers is proudly hosted in collaboration with the Ambassador Crawford College of Business and Entrepreneurship at Kent State University.

This partnership brings together academic excellence, entrepreneurial spirit, and real-world problem-solving to empower high school students to tackle the financial health barriers affecting their communities.

Compete on Kent State's campus, connect with university mentors, and gain exposure to college-level innovation and business thinking.

Pick Your Focus

Four Innovation Tracks

Choose one challenge. Research why it's a barrier to financial health and wealth building. Build a solutionâ€”policy proposal, app prototype, financial literacy tool, community program, or anything else that could help young adults break free from these barriers and start building long-term financial security.

Transportation

Car ownership costs, insurance rates, and public transit gaps prevent young adults from accessing stable jobs and building financial security.

Housing Affordability

Rent burden, security deposits, and credit requirements block young adults from stable housing and long-term wealth building opportunities.

03

Medical debt is a leading cause of bankruptcy. High healthcare costs devastate young adults before their careers even begin.

Simple Process

Three Steps to Compete

1

Register

Your school signs up teams. Individual students or groups welcome (groups split the cash prizes). Choose one innovation track and start exploring.

2

Research & Build

Spend several weeks understanding the barrier and building your solution. Use AI tools to help prototype apps, analyze data, or create content. Submit 3 weeks before the event. Any formatâ€”video demo, working prototype, policy proposal, presentation.

3

Prepare & Present

We'll review submissions and notify you 2 weeks before the event whether you're pitching on stage (7 min + Q&A) or presenting via poster gallery (1-on-1 with judges). Then prepare your presentation format and compete at Kent State.
