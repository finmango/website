Frequently Asked Questions

How will solutions be judged?

We evaluate three things:

  • Thinking Process (40%): Did you use AI to enhance your thinking? Can you explain your research process? Did you evaluate options critically?
  • Solution Quality (40%): Is it actionable? Can others implement it? Does it actually solve the problem? Is there measurable impact?
  • Documentation (20%): Is your process clear? Can someone learn from your approach? Does it pass the "Brain Check"?

We're not looking for perfect solutions‚Äîwe're looking for critical thinking and real problem-solving.

Who can participate?

Young adults ages 18-29. You don't need to be a student‚Äîanyone facing affordability challenges can participate. We encourage submissions from people actually experiencing these problems.

Can I submit as a team?

Yes! Teams of up to 3 people can collaborate. The $500 prize would be split among team members. All team members must be 18-29.

What AI tools can I use?

Any free AI tools: ChatGPT (free tier), Claude, Google Gemini, Perplexity, or others. We focus on free tools so everyone can participate regardless of budget.

What happens to winning solutions?

We publish them publicly (with your permission) so others can benefit. You retain ownership of your work. We may feature winners on our blog and social media. Think of it as building your public portfolio.

Do I get proof of my work for employers?

Yes! All participants receive a certificate of completion. Winners receive a certificate of achievement. Both demonstrate AI problem-solving skills and critical thinking to potential employers.

What if my solution doesn't work perfectly?

That's okay! We value honest documentation of what you tried, what worked, what didn't, and what you learned. Real problem-solving includes failures and iterations.

How many solutions can I submit?

One per person per quarter. We want quality over quantity. Focus on creating one well-researched, thoughtful solution.
