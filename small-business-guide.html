This is a resource for small business owners to efficiently bring their business to the digital era. This guide is free of charge and everything suggested can be done for free‚Äîalthough we also mention paid alternatives. All prices are in US dollars.

We're avid travelers who love supporting local businesses worldwide. Small businesses, especially those outside the USA, sometimes aren't very tech-savvy. We get it‚Äîrunning a small business is demanding! But developing a good online presence is crucial for growth.

Just as a good storefront encourages customers to walk in, a good online presence helps you reach more potential customers. Most businesses benefit from having a website, social media presence, Google Maps listing, and review sites like TripAdvisor or Yelp.

1. Getting Started - Accounts & Security

‚ñº

Almost all services mentioned in this guide require creating accounts. Keep track of which accounts you've created and where.

üîë Create a Google Account for Your Business

We recommend creating a Google account specifically for your business. This provides email, storage, calendar, and more‚Äîall free. Use your business name for the account (e.g., mybusinessname.seattle@gmail.com).

üí° Pro Tip

Dots in your account name are ignored by Google, so you can use them to break up words and make it easier to read: my.business.name@gmail.com

üîê Password Management

Passwords should be difficult to guess, include numbers, letters, and special characters, and never be reused. Here's how to manage them:

  • Use "Sign in with Google" or "Sign in with Facebook" when available‚Äîone less password to remember
  • Use a password manager: Most browsers have built-in password managers. Google Chrome syncs passwords with your Google account. For cross-platform, try LastPass (free for personal use)
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on Google, Facebook, and other important accounts for added security
‚ö†Ô∏è Important

Try to create accounts with as few service providers as possible. Even if you could save money elsewhere, managing everything through fewer providers makes your online presence easier to maintain.

2. Creating a Website

‚ñº

Do you really need a website? Some businesses thrive with just a Facebook page. While social media is great for existing customers, a website is your storefront for all potential customers.

However, websites need maintenance (at least 1-2 hours monthly). An outdated website can be worse than no website. If you can't commit to updates, focus on social media or Google My Business instead.

üåê Free Website Builders

We recommend Google Sites because:

  • No "premium" upsells‚Äîthe free version has all features
  • Simple and highly customizable
  • Free custom domain optional (not required)

To start: Go to sites.google.com on desktop, log in with your Google account, and choose a template from the gallery.

‚úÖ Test Your Website

Before launching, test on:

  • Desktop: Chrome, Safari, and Firefox
  • Mobile: iPhone and Android phones
  • Tablet: iPad or Android tablet

Ask friends and family to test and provide feedback on design and content!

üåü Other Options

Free alternatives with base plans:

  • WordPress
  • Wix
  • Weebly

All-in-one e-commerce: Shopify (monthly fee, but includes website, domain, social media, and payment processing)

Professional agency: Should cost under $1,000 for a custom website. Warning: Review contracts carefully‚Äîsome agencies retain ownership or charge ongoing fees even without updates.

üåç Custom Domain Names

Your Google Sites page will have an address like sites.google.com/view/mybusinessname. For a custom domain (mybusinessname.com):

  • Purchase from Google Domains, CloudFlare, or GoDaddy
  • Cost: $10-14 per year
  • Important: You must renew yearly to keep the domain. If you stop paying, anyone can buy it after expiration
üìß Professional Email

For a professional email (contact@mybusinessname.com), you need a custom domain plus email hosting:

  • Microsoft 365
  • Google Workspace

Cost: $5-10 per month. Use reputable providers to avoid predatory pricing that increases after the first year.

üîç Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

For small businesses, we don't recommend paying for SEO services. Instead:

  • Keep your website updated with relevant information
  • Ensure it loads fast on desktop and mobile
  • Include keywords customers might search ("best Chinese restaurants in Seattle")
  • Search for your business regularly to check visibility

Use Google Search Console to troubleshoot issues and optimize your site's treatment by Google Search.

3. Electronic Payments & E-Commerce

‚ñº

Electronic payments include credit cards, debit cards, and cryptocurrency. E-commerce means selling goods/services online‚Äîeven physical businesses can benefit by offering pre-paid packages or redeemable vouchers.

üí≥ Point of Sale (POS) Systems

To process electronic payments in-person, work with a local bank or payment processor:

  • Square
  • Stripe
  • PayPal

Costs: Under $20/month maintenance + 3-4% + 30-60¬¢ per transaction

üõí Online Payments

For US, UK, and AU businesses: Square Online (no monthly fee for base plan, includes website builder)

Alternative: Shopify (more expensive but integrates with many payment processors including PayPal)

‚ö†Ô∏è Security Warning

Never store customer credit card information. Use payment providers to handle all liability and security. Never handle card info directly if possible.

4. Google My Business & Maps

‚ñº

Google is crucial for potential customers finding your business online. Google My Business helps you manage Google Search, Google Maps, and more.

üìç Google Maps Listing

Essential for physical storefronts! Link to your Google My Business profile to:

  • Edit description and business category
  • Choose which photos appear
  • Reply to user reviews
  • Update hours and contact info
üí° Responding to Reviews

Always be polite and civil when replying to reviews, especially negative ones. Google won't remove negative reviews based on factual disagreements. Flag inappropriate reviews that violate Google's policies.

üåê Google My Business Website

Google offers a simple website builder, but we recommend creating your own website (see Section 2) for more customization. Make sure to link your custom website in your business information.

5. Digital Advertising

‚ñº

Digital advertising delivers promotional messages over the internet. It's a multi-billion dollar industry that enables highly targeted campaigns.

‚ö†Ô∏è Our Recommendation for Small Businesses

We recommend small businesses limit their investment in digital advertising. Why? It requires:

  • Significant time learning advertising strategies
  • Large ongoing time commitment for management
  • Digital ad agencies cost $1,000+ monthly minimum

Instead, focus effort and money on traditional marketing or growing your core business. Once you've grown organically and can dedicate significant budget, then adopt digital advertising.

üìß Email Marketing (Recommended Starting Point)

The simplest form of digital advertising. Encourage website visitors to sign up for your email list/newsletter.

  • Never sign up customers without consent‚Äîit's illegal in many places and gets flagged as spam
  • Use MailChimp (free basic tier, reputable platform)
  • Great for repeat customers (hint: most businesses have them!)
üì± Facebook Ads

One of the most successful platforms for small businesses. Facebook's extensive user data (including Instagram and WhatsApp) enables precise audience targeting.

Learn more: Facebook Ads

üîç Google AdWords

The longest-lived, most successful digital advertising platform. Mainly delivers ads to Google Search users based on what they're searching for.

More complex than Facebook Ads and requires significant knowledge and practice for success.

Learn more: Google AdWords

6. Social Media Presence

‚ñº

Social media allows businesses to interact with potential customers. Like digital advertising, it requires significant ongoing effort but is generally free (cost = your time).

‚è∞ Time Commitment

Monitor your social media channels regularly. If you can't maintain them at least weekly, focus on other areas of your digital presence. Abandoned social media can hurt more than help.

üéØ Social Media ‚â† Advertising

Social media isn't just for promoting‚Äîit's for engaging and building relationships with customers. Different business types use it differently:

  • Restaurant: Post specials or limited-time menu items to attract new customers
  • Bike shop: Post about biking trails to engage existing customers and build long-term relationships
üì± Platform Strategy

Social media platforms change rapidly (rise, fall, demographics shift). Keep up with what your customers are using‚Äîpotentially ignore platforms you're most familiar with.

Each platform has its own conventions:

  • Some expect weekly posts maximum
  • Others accept daily content
  • Don't treat all platforms equally
‚è±Ô∏è Time-Saving Tools

Schedule posts in advance to maintain regular content:

  • Buffer (free for multiple platforms)
  • Later (free for multiple platforms)

Note: There's no substitute for reading what customers say about your business‚Äîthat feedback is valuable!

7. Analytics & Tracking

‚ñº

"If you can't measure it, you can't improve it." Track metrics to ensure your online presence is working.

üìä Website Analytics

We recommend Google Analytics‚Äîone of the most powerful products available, completely free.

  • For Google Sites: Setup instructions
  • For other websites: Visit Google Analytics

Non-Google alternatives:

  • Clicky
  • Bit.ly (only tracks visitors from Bit.ly short links)
  • Facebook Analytics (complex, requires significant time investment)
üì± Social Media Analytics

Most platforms provide built-in analytics:

  • Facebook: Page Insights
  • Instagram, YouTube, etc. have equivalent tools
