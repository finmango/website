1. Overview

The Financial Health Barometer is a composite index measuring economic stress across four dimensions in all 50 US states plus the District of Columbia. The index aggregates data from authoritative government sources to provide a real-time snapshot of financial wellbeing at the state level.

Purpose This tool is designed for educational awareness, policy research, and public understanding of economic stress patterns. It is not intended as a definitive measure but as a directional indicator of relative stress between states.

3. Indicator Calculations

3.1 Financial Anxiety Index

Measures household financial stress based primarily on employment stability and regional economic conditions.

Financial Anxiety = (BASE + (Unemployment Rate ‚àí 3.5) √ó 18) √ó Regional Multiplier

Components:

  • BASE = 120 (baseline index value)
  • Unemployment Rate = Current state unemployment rate from BLS LAUS
  • 3.5% = Baseline "healthy" unemployment rate
  • Regional Multiplier = 0.85‚Äì1.35 based on historical economic patterns

3.2 Food Insecurity Index

Measures food access challenges based on poverty rates and regional economic conditions.

Food Insecurity = (85 + (Poverty Rate ‚àí 10.0) √ó 6) √ó Regional Multiplier

Components:

  • Poverty Rate = State poverty rate from Census SAIPE
  • 10.0% = Baseline national target poverty rate
  • Regional Multiplier = Accounts for regional cost-of-living variations

3.3 Housing Stress Index

Measures housing affordability crisis based on price changes and rent burden.

Housing Stress = (100 + HPI Change √ó 3 + Rent Penalty) √ó Regional Multiplier

Components:

  • HPI Change = Year-over-year housing price index change from FRED
  • Rent Penalty = 15‚Äì45 points based on known high-cost markets:

3.4 Affordability Index

Composite measure combining housing costs and overall economic pressure.

Affordability = (Housing Stress √ó 0.60) + (Food Insecurity √ó 0.40)

5. Limitations & Caveats

Important Limitations Users should be aware of the following limitations when interpreting this data.

Data Lag

  • Unemployment data: 1-month lag (BLS release schedule)
  • Poverty data: 1-2 year lag (Census SAIPE estimates)
  • Housing data: 1-3 month lag (FRED quarterly updates)

Methodological Limitations

  • Weighting: Multipliers are based on research-informed estimates, not empirically derived weights
  • Regional Multipliers: Based on historical patterns that may not reflect current conditions
  • Rent Tiers: State-level classifications may miss within-state variation

Scope Limitations

  • State-level data only; does not capture within-state variation (urban vs. rural)
  • Does not include: healthcare costs, childcare costs, transportation costs
  • Point-in-time estimates without uncertainty bands
