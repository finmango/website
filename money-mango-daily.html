Why a puzzle, not a quiz

Money systems are chain reactions.

A quiz tests whether you know the right answer. A puzzle makes you reason your way to it. This one asks: "given what just happened — what would happen next?" You solve it by thinking through cause and effect, the same way the systems actually work in real life.

01 / Reason

Solvable by thinking.

No financial trivia required. If you can reason through "if A happened, what would happen next?", you can solve it. The system reveals itself as you trace the chain.

02 / System

The chain is the lesson.

One ER bill ripples through six systems: collections, credit, car loans, insurance, housing, and back to your next paycheck. Once you see the cascade, you can't unsee it.

03 / Truth

Each step teaches why.

Every correct placement reveals a one-line truth: the specific policy, market mechanism, or design choice that makes the chain link. Reading the chain is reading the system.
