## 1. The positioning decision

**We are NOT applying as FinMango.** The Academy is built for early-stage founders taking an
AI-powered nonprofit product from MVP to beta. FinMango is a 9-year-old 501(c)(3) with global
reach and a pending SFF long-form application — it reads as too mature for this program, and
applying as FinMango would muddy the SFF story (where FinMango is positioned as an established
research org requesting ~$750K).

**We ARE applying as a new venture that comes out of FinMango:**

- A new AI-native product, founded by Scott Glasgow.
- Fiscally supported/incubated by FinMango (501(c)(3), EIN 81-2543425) during launch only.
- **Committed to spinning out as an independent tech nonprofit** — this matches the Academy's
  eligibility language exactly ("not yet registered, committed to becoming a tech nonprofit in
  the near future").
- Founder story: Scott is transitioning away from FinMango day-to-day operations into a board
  role specifically to build this full-force. This is a *strength* — it answers the "is the
  founder committed?" question before it's asked, and it's the strongest possible signal for a
  program that funds founders, not orgs.

### Why this framing wins

The single hardest thing for MVP→beta founders is **getting real users**. Our unfair advantage:

1. **A built-in classroom beta channel.** FinMango's in-school programming (Millionaire
   Mindset) runs in 27 high schools and colleges via lead instructor Bob Gillingham, reaching
   ~8,000 students per year. The Academy runs Oct 5 – Nov 9 — mid-semester. We can have a live
   classroom beta *during the program itself*, with structured evaluation instruments FinMango
   has run since 2021.
2. **A global community for user research.** FinMango's ambassador network spans ~12 countries,
   plus 100K+ students taught to date. Recruiting testers, running interviews, and gathering
   diverse feedback is a solved problem for us.
3. **Lived experience.** A decade in classrooms watching exactly where financial education
   fails students — engagement dies the moment the instructor leaves the room, and one-shot
   lessons don't survive contact with a student's actual financial life.

> ⚠️ **Number consistency:** the website says "10M+ impacted / 100K+ educated"; the June 2026
> SFF application says "more than a million people / 75,000+ students." Pick one set of figures
> and use it everywhere — funders in this ecosystem overlap and cross-read applications.

## 2. The product (working concept)

**Working name:** *Mango Coach* (placeholder — the FinMango lineage helps in this application;
the spin-out gets its own brand later. Alternatives: Sprout, Compass, Ledger.)

**One-liner:** An AI financial-health coach for teens that lives beyond the classroom — it
starts in a FinMango lesson and keeps coaching the student through their first job, first
paycheck, first credit card, and first investment account.

**Why AI is core (not a workflow tool):** the product IS the coach. It holds a persistent,
personalized conversation with each student, adapts to their actual financial situation
(first job, gig income, family obligations), and escalates to curriculum moments and local
resources (FinMango's NavTech routing) when it detects a need. Remove the AI and there is no
product — it's not a quiz engine with a chatbot bolted on.

**What exists today (be honest in the app):**
- Curriculum and lesson content refined across ~9 years and 100K+ students.
- Evaluation pipeline (student/staff evals running since July 2021).
- Adjacent AI/product proof: Financial Health Predictor, AI-Ready assessment tools, Barometer.
- MVP of the coach itself: **to be built before July 17** — see §5. The application requires a
  3-minute video *demonstrating the product*, so a working demo is non-negotiable.

**Beta plan (the centerpiece of the application):**
- Weeks 1–2 (Oct 5–17): instrument the MVP, finalize consent/privacy flow with 2–3 partner
  teachers.
- SF week (Oct 19): pressure-test the model design and safety approach with FFWD mentors.
- Weeks 4–6 (Oct 26 – Nov 9): live beta in 3–5 of Bob's classrooms (target: 150–400 students),
  daily usage data + structured evals; ambassador-led feedback sessions in 2–3 countries for
  future localization.
- Post-program: expand across the 27-school network in spring semester; incorporate as an
  independent tech nonprofit.

**Safety/trust posture (they will ask):** minors + money = high sensitivity. Commit up front to:
no product recommendations or affiliate anything, no data sale, guardrailed model outputs
reviewed against curriculum, teacher/parent visibility, COPPA/FERPA-aware design. FinMango's
decade of school relationships is the trust moat here.
